        This is the fourth time I've ended up at this restaurant, and it was purely for one reason, and one reason only. I cravedddddd their falafel.

        I can't quite compare this falafel with any other falafel, mainly because this is the only place I've ever purchased and consumed one. But I do say, I'm a picky eater.

        The falafel cost me a whopping $4.09, courtesy of the $1 off yelp check-in coupon.  Cheaper, fresher, and jam-packed with more food than a subway sandwich. Yup. I said it.

        This wrap is well flavored, not too heavy on the seasoning which is a plus in my book. It came with three sauces on the side, I believe it was taziki sauce, hot sauce, and hummus (not really a sauce right?!?) Goodness gracious, it was fantastic.

        Though I finished the phenomenal falafel in one sitting, I'm almost certain it was made to feed two of me.

        All in all, the falafels here are delicious, inexpensive, and filling.

        Oh yeah, and they have pretty bomb baklava.

        Tried it for the first time a couple of weeks ago and liked it enough to return twice, and I'll be… Tried it for the first time a couple of weeks ago and liked it enough to return twice, and I'll be back again regularly, for as long as the quality persists. I've had the chicken gyro platter, the gyro platter, the quabuli pallow with lamb, the chapli kabob, and the squash bolani. All of it was quite good, and I'd have any of it again. The place is no frills in terms of decor, etc., which allows them to focus their attention on the food. It's great to (finally!) have a place in Hayward that makes Afghan food. I really hope the place succeeds, because Hayward desperately needs a place like this. However, I think they need to do a better job marketing themselves. For example, they need a website with a complete and current menu, days/hours of operation, location and direction, and some good pictures of the place and some of the food. Normally it makes me nervous to see such a wide menu (pizza, burgers, fries, Afghan, Greek, etc.) because it's impossible to do everything at a high quality level, especially with such a small operation. But everything I've had so far has been good. One tip is that you shouldn't go when you're in a rush. The food takes longer to arrive than you'd expect. However, I'd much rather they take their time and focus on quality than rush to speed along the delivery, getting sloppy. I hope this works out; they have a pretty good location and don't have any immediate competitors for this type of food until Fremont/Newark, so if they can advertise themselves throughout Hayward, they have the potential to do well, especially given how much of a wasteland Hayward is in terms of restaurants.
