        If I were to sum up this restaurant in one sentence: Contemporary sushi restaurant that is trying… If I were to sum up this restaurant in one sentence: Contemporary sushi restaurant that is trying too hard to be fancy.

        When I came to eat here, they were giving out a bunch of freebies. In total, I got 3. A crab salad once I sat down, the option of a free appetizer (I picked the tuna nachos) and an amuse bouche.

        I ordered the dynamite mussels, a riceless roll, and a sashimi omakase.

        I really felt that they were going over the top with their presentation, sauces, flavor profile, etc. A lot of their combinations didn't go together.

        For example the amuse bouche was pickled daikon wrapped around a piece of hamachi sashimi, with sirachia mayo and unagi sauce. Did not work.

        The tuna nachos...tempura battered and deep fried shiso leaf with a deep fried won ton skin, with the spicy tuna/siracha mix, plus more sauces and garnishes. Too much.

        The sashimi omakase was plated in a very loud fashion, not with the subtle delicacy of authentic Japanese food. There were generous cuts of fish, mainly salmon, hamachi, and tuna. They were drowned in a olive oil, soy sauce, and ponzu sauce, topped with tobiko, onion, garlic, seaweed salad, and a flower. The flavors didn't really go together, and for an omakase plate, the variety wasn't much to speak of.

        I'd say that they have generous portions and friendly service. The items I ordered seemed reasonably fresh as well. If they could refine the flavor profile of their food and learn the "less is more" colloquialism, they could go from a restaurant with mediocre food to a restaurant with very tasty food.

        Otherwise, if you like butchered up Japanese food covered in lots of sauce and fancy looking bits, this is your place.         Read more
