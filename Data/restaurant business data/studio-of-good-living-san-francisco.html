        I bought a Living Social deal for a vegetarian cooking class offered by Studio of Good Living. The class is held in Phoebe's home, and in our particular class there were 10 people. we quickly reviewed the recipes we would be making that night, and then went into the kitchen to start cooking. Unfortunately, that kitchen is too small for 10 people. You're sharing space, knives, woks, cutting boards, burners (I cooked on a hotplate) and its just too chaotic. Phoebe was running around grabbing ingredients that we needed so there really wasn't any instruction; we read the recipes of the sheets of paper and figured it out. On that note: I didn't really feel like she was particularly friendly or approachable during class, and she seemed really overwhelmed.

        I learned two things that night: one was to add a little bit of yogurt to Indian dishes after they're done cooking to cut the heat, and the other was that if you add cornstarch to thicken something bring it to a boil/simmer after you add it or the food will get watery. Cool tips, but I wish there had been more. I would really say this was more of a "have a dinner party with strangers" evening than it was a cooking class. The recipes tasted good though.

        I'm glad I got the class half-off on Living Social and don't plan on doing it again. Also suggest that they have a survey on their site or ask for anonymous feedback after dinner so that as she tries to grow her business she can get an idea of the experience participants have...

      Specialties

      Studio of Good Living is the San Francisco Bay Area's only one stop Culinary Resource Center. We offer Gourmet Meal Delivery Service customized to you and your family's dietary needs and professionally prepared and delivered to your home. We also specialize in Event Catering Services, Private Chef Services, Customized Cooking Classes and Food Tours. Whether you need us for that once-in-a-lifetime event or want to engage with The Studio of Good Living on a weekly basis, our flexibility and personal attention to our clients is our top focus.

      History

      Established in 2005.

      After leaving the restaurant world, Chef Phoebe started Studio of Good Living as a specialty private chef and has grown her business in the SF Bay Area to include meal delivery drop off, cooking classes, food tours, personal chef services and private event catering. Her partner, Perrie Bruene joined forces to build upon and broaden the culinary services they love to create and share with others. "Food is OUR Passion!"

      Meet the Business Owner

      Phoebe S.
      Business Owner

      Bruce Springsteen may have been born to run but baby, I was born to cook! I started cooking at 7 with my mom and great Aunt Lily. I attended college for a year but dropped out when I got the opportunity to study at Le Cordon Bleu in Paris. I flew to Paris the day after my 19th birthday and I haven't looked back! After returning to the US, I began my restaurant career and after several years in restaurants I decided to gain more knowledge and expand my horizon. With that in mind I enrolled in the Culinary Institute of America in Hyde Park, NY. I have worked in restaurants in NY, Maine and the Island of St. John. In 1999 I found a new home in the San Francisco Bay Area and in 2005 I established the Studio of Good Living.  Looking at food and my work as a chef differently, I wanted to work with people and teach them about their own nutrition and how to incorporate that knowledge into delicious meals and snacks that they enjoy eating. Visit us at the Studio of Good Living Today!
