        My family always comes here whenever we are in Sunnyvale. The food here is average at best; it's decent when I'm eating, but I never have cravings for it.

        I've tried a lot of dishes on their menu and in my opinion, the best ones are the Sichuan dishes. The pickled cabbage and fish pot is insanely spicy but full of flavor. The heat slowly creeps up on you; it's fine for the first few minutes and then all of a sudden, your mouth is on fire! The fish is super tender, the rice noodles soak up so much flavor, and the broth is great. The pickled cabbage (pao cai) is also a solid appetizer and is a nice complement to the main dishes.

        There is really nothing really special about this place. For me, I can never find something I want to eat on the menu. The service is spotty (as expected from any Chinese restaurant). Prices are reasonable. The best part about this restaurant is it's location in downtown Sunnyvale.

        I like this place.  As with any other restaurant my dad latches on to, we always end up ordering the same couple of dishes.

        AMBIANCE: Nothing special.  Tables are decently spaced apart, but sometimes you'll still end up bumping up against your neighbors' backs.  The most recent time we were there, there were fruit flies buzzing around our table-- very very annoying.

        SERVICE: Super friendly.  Several of the staff treat my parents like family friends... which I suppose in a way they are (discovered after several visits that they're from the same home town).  Since you probably won't have that same connection, I'd say that the service here is good; they have more patience than what you'll find at the average Chinese restaurant.

        FOOD: My dad knows what he likes.  We always order the following (sorry, this is a rough English translation as we always order in Chinese):
        - Spicy eggplant
        - Braised catfish
        - Home-style tofu
        - Pickled veggies & fried prawns
        - Green veggie du jour

        Everything is pretty tasty, but nothing is STELLAR.  Sometimes certain dishes are prepared better than others.  They are consistent if nothing else.  The major negatives I can think of is that the food tends to be on the greasy side (even the green veggies) and are sometimes too salty.

        OVERALL: A decent choice in DT Sunnyvale.  I would say that if it were my first visit, I would rate Chef Yu around 3-3.5 stars, but what with the history my family has with it, it gets 4.  Prices are average for the area, and dinner for 4 usually costs under $80 including tax+tip.

        Heads up: Chef Yu is a sleeper cell.  On the surface, he's a sleepy, bland Chinese hole-in-the-wall typical of American surburbia.  He's got the kung pao chicken, the fried rice, the sweet n sour this, and the moo shu that.
